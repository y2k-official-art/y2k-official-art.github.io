Lightfall

2022

electronic part, rock, soil, glass, lightbox, UV tube, full-spectrum fluorescent tube

Lightfall consists of several stereoscopic landscape paintings of vegetation and rocks hung on the wall. Featuring these fictional scenes constructed with fluorescent minerals, this work tries to decipher the “code” written collectively by light and matter.

The fluorescent minerals and plants are exposed to the alternating full-spectrum white (qua-si-daylight) or ultraviolet (UV) light. We can see the imbricated rocks covered with lush green moss during the “daytime.” When the “night” falls, the UV light triggers the electronic transitions in the minerals that add peculiar colors to the rocks, while the plants with chlorophyll glow dimly in red.

The reflector focuses these changes onto each device, below which a corresponding quantitative spectrum is produced via the spectrometer. This rainbow-like “code” is actually the quantification of the colored light in the scene, suggesting that the code is generated by light, and the light is determined by the mass of the object.
