Photolysis

2022

electronic computer, electronic part, rock, plant, glass, lightbox, mirror, optical fiber, culture medium, electroretinogram system

Photolysis is a hybrid installation of bio-photo-receptors following a dynamic cycle. The human being, plankton, and vegetation in this work interact with one another by means of light.

Each of them repeatedly encrypts and decrypts the light information according to their respective photo-sensitive capabilities and algorithmic logics. Configuring the varying irritability of different species and using light as the vehicle for bridging heterogeneous objects, this work encapsulates the physical universe from a biological perspective, and reexamines the way in which human beings observe the world and send feedback.

In addition to comprising photons charged with energy, light carries a piece of information in the electromagnetic spectrum, a dead ringer for a string of secret codes. Covering the full spectrum, a ray of sunshine drives the entire installation. This ray of light is translated by the human electroretinogram and the biological effect of euglena, resulting in the colored light of specific wave-lengths emitted from the electronically controlled lightbox.

The optical fiber projects the resulted data onto the other side of the centered rock. The sunlight and colored light of specific wavelengths lead to the disparate tropism of the plants, which gives diametrically opposite forms to both sides of the centered rock and sparks the imagination of a multi-faceted world.
