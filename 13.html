Synplant

Synplant is an installation consisting of multiple plant hybrid sensing units. Through technical intervention to analyze biosignals, Plant-Environment Interactions can be visualized through cymatics patterns. This work is based on an artificial intelligence-based learning system that analyzes biosignals changes when plants receive environmental stimulations. Such analysis results can drive acoustic-lighting device to generate variable cymatics patterns. Through the utilization of wind and rain as vehicles to bridge different sensing units and generate stimulants to trigger sounds and lights, the audience can perceive the trajectories of the invisible interaction of different elements in the natural environment. This work is proceeded with a post-human perspective, combining machine with a botanical perception viewpoint to extend our understanding and broaden our horizons regarding the natural environment.

Synplant is divided into two parts: 1) biological signal detection and analysis and 2) cymatics visualization of plant-environment interaction. We developed a bioamplifier to collect biosignals and applied an artificial intelligence-based learning system to analyze how plants perceive wind and rain stimuli.

To visualize the variable environmental stimulus that plants perceive, we developed an installation consisting of multiple plant hybrid sensing units. Each sensing unit comprises a plant equipped with a bioamplfier, a Raspberry Pi, and an acoustic-lighting device. The resonance speaker in the acoustic-lighting device vibrates the water in the petri dish by using the interpreted environmental state to generate various cymatics patterns. This physical interpretation method allows audiences to further pay attention to the dark and light connectedness of the array of sensing units; such connectedness is driven by wind and rain and hence provides spectators with a traceable trajectory of Plant-Environment Interaction.

The exhibition layout of Synplant is composed of multiple plant hybrid sensing units arranged in an outdoor space

Recent artworks based on biological technology have been changing the perspective to that of animals, algae, and plants, creating new ideas to debate the issues of the subjectivity of non-human organisms beyond the frameworks of human-centered thinking. In this work, we expand our focus from the ability of a single plant to perceive its environment to reveal the interaction between the plant community and the natural environment. Through artificial intelligence algorithms, we can analyze the different perceptual states of plants through biosignals and provide audiences with a unique perspective of experiencing the natural environment in various cymatics patterns. This work is proceeded with a post-human perspective, combining machine with a botanical perception viewpoint to extend our understanding and broaden our horizons regarding the natural environment. We envision that the artificial intelligence-based plant perception system can  present a multifaceted perceptual experience for human beings through more artistic expressions in the future.
