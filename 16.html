Lucid Dream

The recent progress of modern science has enabled us to detect the physiological status of organisms through their bioelectrical activity; this technique has been constantly applied to contemporary media art. However, exploring plants as a subject and allowing them to interpolate and hence perform art through biosignals remain to be further explored.

Lucid Dream is an artwork in which inspection, displacement, and engagement of plant communities take place, shedding light on the subjective perception of plants, a peripheral subject that rarely came to people’s attention. To achieve this, we entered a forest and applied an artificial intelligence-based learning system that can understand and interpret the local plants' perception of the wind and rain stimulation through their biosignals. Based on this system, we build an environmental-controlled space in the art museum where natural elements perceived by the plants are simulated and artistically represented by artificial machines driven by the plant-nature interaction model. When visitors walk into this space, they can experience the nature perceived by plants through engagements with plants' life from a non-human perspective.

Lucid Dream not only explores how to utilize intelligent computational technologies to understand the perceptual system of plants but also creates sensory experiences across species to extend our understanding and broaden our horizons regarding the natural environment.
