Lucid Dream

2022

electronic computer, electronic part, ambient control system, conduit, mist maker, water tank

Lucid Dream immerses viewers in a dream-like world within the perception of plants, where they experience the drenching of rain and the brush of the wind.

The recent progress of modern science has enabled us to detect the physiological status of organisms through their bioelectrical activity. This technology continues to be widely utilized across disciplines in artistic practice. We focus on how to use biotechnology to intervene in plants and use them as the subject of artistic expression, thereby expanding people's exploration of other species.

Lucid Dream is an artwork in which inspection, displacement, and engagement of plant communities take place, shedding light on the subjective perception of plants, a peripheral subject that rarely came to people’s attention. To achieve this, we applied an artificial intelligence-based learning system that can understand and interpret the plants' perception of the wind and rain stimulation in the forest through their biosignals.

With this system, we build an environmental control space within an art museum where the natural elements perceived by the plants are simulated and artistically represented by artificial machines driven by the plant-nature interaction model. This work immersive the audience in the natural environment that plants perceive, allowing the audience to experience nature from a non-human perspective. This work not only explores how intelligent computational technologies can be utilized to understand the perceptual system of plants but also creates cross-species perceptual experiences that expand our understanding and imagination of new ecosystems.
